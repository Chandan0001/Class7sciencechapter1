Question1: Justify the following statements:

a. Crow is an omnivore:

Crows are considered omnivores because they consume both plant and animal matter. Their diet includes seeds, fruits, insects, small animals, and even waste products, showcasing their ability to eat a wide range of food sources.

b. It is said that the mode of nutrition in human beings and Amoeba is quite similar: :

Both humans and Amoeba utilize holozoic nutrition, where food is ingested (take (food, drink, or another substance) into the body by swallowing), digested, absorbed, assimilated, and waste is egested. Although the processes occur at different levels of complexity, the fundamental steps of nutrition are similar in both.


Question2: Give reasons for the following:

(a) Ingestion of food is difficult without teeth:

Teeth play a crucial role in breaking down food into smaller, manageable pieces, making it easier to swallow and digest. Without teeth, the process of mechanically breaking down food would be difficult, making ingestion challenging.

(b) If we chew rice or bread for a few minutes, it starts tasting sweet.

When rice or bread is chewed, the enzyme amylase present in saliva begins to break down the starches into simpler sugars like maltose. This conversion makes the food taste sweeter after prolonged chewing.

(c) Bacteria are present in the caecum of ruminants:

Bacteria in the caecum of ruminants help in the digestion of cellulose by fermenting plant material. These bacteria play a vital role in breaking down complex carbohydrates that the animal's own digestive enzymes cannot process.


Question3: Explain how digested food gets absorbed into the blood.

Answer:

After digestion in the small intestine, nutrients such as amino acids, simple sugars, and fatty acids are absorbed through the intestinal walls. These nutrients enter the blood capillaries or lymphatic vessels in the villi, small finger-like projections in the intestinal lining. The absorbed nutrients are then transported by the bloodstream to various cells of the body for use in growth, energy, and repair.
